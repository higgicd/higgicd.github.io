# Research Projects

I am currently looking for graduate students interested in: 
- modelling the [urban volumetrics](https://doi.org/10.1177%2F0042098020936970) of 21st-century cities
- 3D/4D econometrics along the lines of these research papers: [one](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.landurbplan.2018.12.011) [two](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.landusepol.2019.03.002) [three](https://doi.org/10.1177/0042098017712680)
- travel behaviour and accessibility in topographically-rich urban environments along the lines of this [paper](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jtrangeo.2019.102564) and [toolbox](https://github.com/higgicd/3D_Network_Toolbox)

Skill sets for each project differ, but candidates should ideally have a background in GIS and spatial analysis with some mix of quantitative or econometric methods and/or experience (or willingness to learn) with R/Python. Strong candidates are eligible for generous additional funding on top of our department packages.

Get in touch and see our [Graduate studies page](https://geography.utoronto.ca/graduate-geography/) and the [School of Graduate Studies](https://www.sgs.utoronto.ca) for more information on how to apply.
